In Loving Memory of Eli McDonald

Forever in our hearts. Always remembered.

Today we gather to say goodbye to Eli, a young man taken from us far too soon at just 15 years old. As a proud member of the Aboriginal community, Eli carried with him the spirit of his ancestors and a connection to Country that stayed with him throughout his short but meaningful life. A student at St Joseph's Regional College (SJRC), Eli was a familiar face in our hallways. Though he hadn't yet started his journey into the workforce, his "job" in this life was much more important: he was a son, a grandson, a classmate, and a friend. He brought a unique presence to every room he walked into, leaving behind memories that his peers and teachers will hold onto dearly. We remember Eli for his personality and the simple things that made him who he was. Whether he was joking around at school or spending time with his mob, he lived life at his own pace. While he may not have been a man of the sea or one for the boats, he found his own ways to navigate the world around him with the love of those who supported him. Eli, you leave a hole in our hearts that cannot be filled. We find comfort knowing you are now at peace, resting with the Elders who went before you. Rest in peace, Eli. You will never be forgotten.
